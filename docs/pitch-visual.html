Visual brief · May 2026

Heart-rate wearables are blind to lifting — you can grind your muscles and CNS to failure with barely a flicker in HR, so they report you hardly trained. meVBT counts every rep and tracks intra-set velocity loss — a validated proximity-to-failure signal — to build the muscular strain & recovery score those platforms structurally can’t.

4 commercial tools → 1 model 3-way agreement to ~0.02 m/s confidence-scored, per rep watch-first, no bar hardware

01 Why now — the blind spot

The same hard set, seen two ways. HR barely moves; the muscular cost — captured by rep-by-rep velocity decay — is enormous. That gap is the product.

03 How it works — one model, many sensors

Every source emits the same shape — rep boundaries, a velocity profile, range of motion, and a confidence — into one fusion layer. A learned personal prior strengthens with every set, so even a lone noisy watch stays legible.

05 Five truths from the first sets

  • Every “ground truth” disagrees — on rep count and decline shape.
  • The reps that matter most are measured worst — the grindy finish carries the fatigue signal.
  • Time-domain metrics stay robust when velocity gets noisy on terminal reps.
  • Confidence is open white space — a leader’s “100%” badge stayed 100% while it missed half a set.
  • Conditions, not category, set accuracy — frame rate & plate shape quietly break video.
  • Calibration is a constant offset — fixable once, per source × lift.
