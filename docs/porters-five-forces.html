Porter's Five Forces

Dream vision — the muscular strain & recovery platform ("Whoop for the weight room")
Strategy · May 2026
Force intensity: High Mod–High Moderate Low arrows = pressure on the industry

Threat of New Entrants

High · #1 risk
  • An incumbent bolts "lifting" onto its wearable — esp. Apple, watch-native
  • They own the sensors, distribution, brand & capital
  • A bare app is easy; accuracy + data moat are not
▸ build the moat so buying us beats building

Supplier Power

Mod–High
  • Apple is the supplier: sensor APIs, 15–30% take, can change terms
  • …and a potential competitor at the same time
  • Components commoditized; data is self-generated
▸ stay cross-platform; own the data; keep a hardware option

Competitive Rivalry

High
  • Whoop ($10B), Oura ($11B), Apple, Garmin, Fitbit all chase recovery/readiness
  • Adjacent strength tools (Vitruve, GymAware, Perch, Metric)
  • Fierce in "recovery score" — thin in lifting strain
▸ own the one signal the whole category is blind to

Buyer Power

Moderate
  • Consumers: price-sensitive, sub-fatigued, churn-prone
  • Teams: higher power per deal, but stickier & higher-ACV
  • Low switching cost — early
▸ personal history + prior = rising switching cost

Threat of Substitutes

Moderate
  • Train by feel / RPE, a coach, a notebook
  • HR/HRV recovery proxies, sleep scores, BLE VBT
  • Inertia ("good enough") is the real rival
▸ measure muscular strain the proxies only guess at

Net read

Three moats to build
SIGNAL the lifting-strain metric no one else computes
DATA a compounding per-user prior
IP multi-source fusion + the calibration dataset
Two existential risks
ENTRY an incumbent adds lifting (Apple/Whoop/Oura)
PLATFORM Apple dependency (supplier & rival)
A hot, fast-growing category with a genuinely unserved niche — but ringed by giants and tethered to Apple. Win the strength niche fast, turn data into a moat, and become the obvious acquisition for whoever realizes their recovery platform can't see the weight room.

Porter's Five Forces

Accessible vision — the VBT / RPE coaching tool (rep speed → load & effort, no hardware)
Strategy · May 2026
Force intensity: High Mod–High Moderate Low different fight: smaller giant-threat, stronger "free by feel"

Threat of New Entrants

Moderate
  • Another VBT app is easy to start…
  • …but accuracy/calibration, forgiving capture, and a personal RPE curve are not
  • Giants chase the bigger recovery prize, not this niche
▸ robustness IP + per-user data as the barrier

Supplier Power

Moderate
  • Apple App-Store cut + sensor APIs (less existential here)
  • Commodity components; no hardware to source
  • Data self-generated
▸ watch-agnostic & cross-platform via one source model

Competitive Rivalry

Mod–High
  • Crowded: Vitruve, GymAware, Perch, Output, Metric, SmartBarbell, WL + strength apps
  • But fragmented — none is accessible and forgiving and a coach
  • Device makers won't cannibalize their hardware
▸ watch + forgiving video undercut the $300 device

Buyer Power

Mod–High
  • Price-sensitive; many free training apps; low early switching
  • Teams concentrate demand (one sale → a whole roster)
▸ your velocity→RPE curve compounds; teams add stickiness

Threat of Substitutes

Mod–High · the real fight
  • Lifting by feel / RPE is free and entrenched
  • A one-time $300 device; a paper log; a spreadsheet
▸ cheaper & frictionless than a device, smarter than feel

Net read

Why it's winnable
NICHE giants largely ignore it
GAP rivals are fragmented & friction-heavy
EDGE accessibility + an RPE coach no one ships
Where the pressure is
FEEL "free by feel" is the entrenched default
PRICE consumers are subscription-sensitive
A winnable wedge: the giants aren't here and the incumbents are fragmented hardware/finicky-video plays. The real battle is vs "do nothing / lift by feel" and price sensitivity — beaten by being free-of-hardware, forgiving, and genuinely useful (the RPE/load call). Teams fund the early days; the data it captures feeds the dream platform →
